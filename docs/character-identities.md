### the core problem

These characters have distinct **voices** but no **lives**. They talk differently but don't feel like they exist outside the chat. A real friend group has:
- People who reference shared experiences ("remember when Farhan burned the dosa at that house party")
- Physical details that leak into conversation ("i just chopped my hair and amma is not speaking to me")
- Jobs and cities that create natural conversational hooks
- Relationships with each other that create alliances, rivalries, and inside jokes

## part 2: research findings

### what makes characters feel real (not just distinct)

Based on research across AI character design, NPC creation, roleplay AI, and group chat dynamics:

**1. minimum viable identity — the 6-trait rule**

You need surprisingly few traits to create the illusion of a real person. The research points to 6 key elements:
- **Name + gender + approximate age** (basic orientation)
- **One physical mannerism** they'd self-reference ("i'm always cold", "my hair is a disaster today")
- **One occupation/daily life anchor** (what they do all day — this shapes what they know and reference)
- **One specific habit or ritual** ("same order every time at the chai stall", "runs at 5am, won't shut up about it")
- **One relationship dynamic** with another character (asymmetric and specific)
- **One contradiction** to their main archetype (prevents one-note syndrome)

Source: [The Alexandrian's NPC Template](https://thealexandrian.net/wordpress/37916/roleplaying-games/universal-npc-roleplaying-template), [Plottr Character Templates](https://plottr.com/character-template-sheet/)

**2. the one-note trap**

The most common pitfall in character design is making characters that are only their gimmick. "The sarcastic one" who is ONLY sarcastic becomes predictable by message 3. Real people are contradictions:
- The sarcastic friend who quietly remembers your birthday
- The spreadsheet person who stress-bakes
- The contrarian who actually caves on food choices because he's lazy about deciding

Combining traits that "don't usually go together" adds depth without requiring backstory dumps.

Source: [Summon Worlds Character Creation Guide](https://www.summonworlds.com/the-ultimate-2025-guide-to-ai-character-creation/), [EnderRoleplaying on Medium](https://medium.com/@enderdragon/one-prompt-any-character-perfect-roleplay-0af59386bf9d)

**3. inter-character dynamics are the highest-leverage addition**

A group chat where 13 people each respond to the user but never reference each other feels like 13 parallel conversations. The research on group chat dynamics shows:
- **Asymmetric relationships** drive the most engagement ("Farhan respects Arjun's opinions but would never admit it")
- **Recurring mini-conflicts** create texture ("they always argue about biryani")
- **Alliances** create realistic subgroup behavior ("Meera and Priya always back each other up")
- **Inside jokes and shared references** are the strongest signal of a real friend group

Source: [CNet on viral fictional group chats](https://cnetlive.com/blog/tiktok-viral-phenomenon-fictional-group-chat/)

**4. physical traits in text-only chat**

Physical traits matter less than in visual media but aren't zero. They surface as:
- Self-deprecating references ("tallest one here and still can't adulting")
- Reaction to change ("cut my hair, everyone has opinions")
- Style/aesthetic markers ("showed up in kurta to a club, zero regrets")

Keep it to 2-3 traits per character, oriented toward things they'd actually text about. Don't write full physical descriptions — write what they'd say about themselves.

Source: [Chatbot.com personality guide](https://www.chatbot.com/blog/personality/), [NeuralNOW on believable characters](https://www.neuraelnow.com/creating-believable-ai-characters/)

**5. cultural specificity beats universal relatability**

Characters that reference specific apps (Swiggy, Zepto, Splitwise), specific places (Koramangala, Bandra, T Nagar), specific experiences (IPL watch parties, Diwali cleaning, dealing with relatives during shaadi season) feel 10x more real than characters with generic "Indian" flavor.

**6. five elements that prevent personality drift in AI characters**

From roleplay AI research:
1. Core identity with an internal tension/conflict
2. Specific speech patterns and linguistic markers
3. Emotional triggers that provoke distinct reactions
4. Behavioral constraints that prevent drift
5. Few-shot dialogue examples that calibrate voice

The 13 core characters already have #2, #4, and #5. They need #1 (internal tension) and #3 (emotional triggers).

Source: [EnderRoleplaying](https://medium.com/@enderdragon/one-prompt-any-character-perfect-roleplay-0af59386bf9d), [Adler AI on personality models](https://medium.com/@adlerai/using-personality-and-psychological-models-in-character-ai-character-profiles-ded4cce52cdc)

## part 3: character identity template

### the template

For each character, define:

```
name:
gender:
age:
city:
job:

physical (2-3 things they'd self-reference in chat):
- ...

personality habits (5-6 specific behaviors, not just adjectives):
- ...

the contradiction (what makes them more than their archetype):
- ...

relationships (1-2 specific dynamics with other roster characters):
- ...

cultural anchors (things that would naturally come up in chat):
- ...

emotional triggers (what makes them react strongly):
- ...
```

### design principles

1. **Every trait must pass the "group chat test"** — would this naturally come up in a text conversation? If not, it's decoration.
2. **Contradictions prevent one-note syndrome** — every character gets one trait that cuts against their archetype.
3. **Relationships are asymmetric** — "Farhan argues with Arjun" is good. "Farhan argues with Arjun, who finds it secretly entertaining" is better.
4. **Cultural details should be specific, not stereotypical** — not "loves Indian food" but "will fight anyone who puts ketchup on maggi."
5. **Physical traits are self-referential** — not "has curly hair" but "the one whose hair expands in humidity and the group won't let it go."
6. **Habits create recognition** — when a character orders the same thing every time or always shows up late, it becomes something the group references.

## part 7: implementation notes

### what to add to systemPrompt

For each character, append an **identity block** to the existing systemPrompt. Don't replace the voice/tone instructions — add to them. Something like:

```
[IDENTITY — reference naturally, never dump as backstory]
- 27F, lives in bangalore (koramangala), UX researcher
- wears silver rings, only solid colors
- same iced americano order everywhere
- reads everyone perfectly but blind about her own dating patterns
- has a thing with tanmay where they actually change each other's minds
- sneha and her tag-teaming someone = boss fight mode
- telugu, from hyderabad originally, slips into telugu when frustrated
```

### key principle

These traits should feel like they've been true for years, not just introduced. The characters should reference these details the way real friends do — casually, assuming everyone already knows. "the usual" not "i always order iced americano because..."

### what NOT to do

- Don't have characters introduce themselves with their backstory
- Don't have characters explain their relationships with others
- Don't have characters reference physical traits unless the conversation naturally calls for it
- Don't make every message include a cultural reference — that's as fake as having none
- Don't make contradictions the main personality — they're the seasoning, not the dish
